I am a Ph.D. student (direct enrollment) at the School of Computer Science and Technology, Xi'an Jiaotong University, starting in 2026. Before that, I was an undergraduate at Hefei University of Technology. My research focuses on computer vision, particularly low-light image super-resolution and hyperspectral image reconstruction.

I am broadly interested in building robust vision models that can recover fine structural and spectral details under challenging imaging conditions. Specifically, my recent work explores: (1) physics-informed degradation modeling for real-world super-resolution; (2) plug-and-play spectral refinement modules for hyperspectral imaging; (3) learning with limited paired data via semi-supervised and unsupervised strategies. Please feel free to reach out if you are interested in related topics or potential collaborations.

📑 Research Projects

📄 Dual-Path Learning based on Frequency Structural Decoupling and Regional-Aware Fusion for Low-Light Image Super-Resolution

IEEE International Conference on Multimedia & Expo (ICME), 2026 ICME 2026

A three-stage low-light image super-resolution framework. (FSD) separates the input into low-frequency luminance and high-frequency texture components. (SDR) contains a bio-inspired luminance enhancer and a hierarchical texture denoiser. (CSR) fuses enhanced luminance and restored texture to reconstruct the final high-resolution output. Trained and evaluated on the RELLISUR dataset.

📄 SR²-Net: A General Plug-and-Play Model for Spectral Refinement in Hyperspectral Image Super-Resolution

Under review, IJCAI 2026

A plug-and-play spectral refinement module that integrates seamlessly with existing HSI super-resolution frameworks. We design a hierarchical spectral self-attention (H-S³A) mechanism and a multi-scale channel refinement (MCR) block to recover fine spectral details.
